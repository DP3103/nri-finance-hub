Understanding TDS for NRIs: 2025 Guide

Tax Deducted at Source (TDS) is a major concern for many Non-Resident Indians (NRIs). This guide explains when TDS applies, how to reclaim it, and the latest rates.

What is TDS and When Is It Due?

TDS is a tax deducted before income is paid to you. For example, banks deduct TDS from interest before crediting your account.

  • Interest income
  • Dividends
  • Rental income
  • Capital gains from shares, mutual funds, real estate, and other assets

Can NRIs Reclaim TDS?

Yes! If you’ve paid more TDS than required, file an income tax return to reclaim the excess. Ensure correct deductions to avoid penalties.

Tip: If TDS isn’t deducted, authorities may impose penalties. Always verify your income under Indian law.

TDS Applicability for NRIs

TDS applies to most Indian-sourced income, such as:

  • Interest from bank deposits and bonds
  • Stock dividends
  • Capital gains (mutual funds, shares, property sales)

Exemption: Interest on NRE and FCNR deposits is usually exempt.
